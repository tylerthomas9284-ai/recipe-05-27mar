Smashed Avocado Toast with Poached Eggs

Perfectly poached eggs with silky, jammy yolks on charred sourdough with herb-smashed avocado, chili flakes, and a drizzle of your best olive oil. The definitive version — no shortcuts.

About This Recipe

There are a lot of bad avocado toasts in the world: mushy avocado spread from a package, flimsy sandwich bread, overcooked eggs with chalky yolks. This is not that. This recipe treats each component with the attention it deserves — properly ripe avocado smashed (not blended) with just enough lemon and salt, charred sourdough from a good bakery loaf, and poached eggs with that glossy, barely-set white and a warm, flowing yolk.

The poaching technique we use here is the simplest and most reliable: a gentle swirl, no vinegar, fresh eggs. The difference between a fresh egg and a week-old egg in poaching is significant — fresh whites hold together around the yolk; older whites spread into wisps. Check the "best by" date and use the freshest eggs you can find.

Ingredients

  • 2 thick slices sourdough bread (about 1.5 inches thick)
  • 2 large very fresh eggs
  • 1 large ripe Hass avocado
  • 1 tablespoon fresh lemon juice
  • ¼ teaspoon flaky sea salt, plus more to finish
  • Small handful fresh cilantro or flat-leaf parsley, roughly chopped
  • ½ teaspoon white wine vinegar (for the poaching water)
  • Chili flakes or Aleppo pepper, to taste
  • Extra-virgin olive oil, to drizzle
  • Optional: cherry tomatoes, microgreens, or Everything Bagel seasoning

Method

Smash the avocado
Halve the avocado, remove the pit, and scoop the flesh into a bowl. Add lemon juice and flaky salt. Using a fork, smash to your preferred texture — we go for roughly half-smooth, half-chunky. Fold in the fresh herbs. Taste and adjust seasoning. The avocado should taste quite seasoned on its own; it will mellow once on the toast.
Char the sourdough
Toast the sourdough slices in a cast iron skillet or grill pan over high heat with a tiny drizzle of olive oil for 2–3 minutes per side, until deep golden with charred edges. Alternatively, use a broiler set to high — 2 minutes per side on a rack close to the element. You want proper char, not just toasted. While hot, rub each slice with a cut garlic clove for an extra layer of flavor (optional but recommended).

Poach the eggs
Fill a medium saucepan with about 3 inches of water. Add the white wine vinegar. Bring to a gentle simmer — you want small, lazy bubbles, not a rolling boil. Crack each egg into a separate small ramekin or cup. Create a gentle swirl in the water with a spoon. Slide one egg gently into the center of the swirl. The white will wrap around the yolk naturally. Poach for exactly 3 minutes for a runny yolk, 3:30 for slightly jammy. Remove with a slotted spoon and drain on a paper towel. Repeat for the second egg.
Assemble and finish
Spread the smashed avocado generously over each slice of toast — be lavish. Place a poached egg on top of each. Sprinkle with flaky sea salt, chili flakes, and a pinch of freshly ground black pepper. Drizzle with a generous amount of your best olive oil. Scatter any optional toppings. Eat immediately — this is not a dish that waits.

Perfect Poaching Every Time: The most common mistake is water that's too hot — violently boiling water tears the white apart. Aim for a bare simmer with just a few bubbles breaking the surface. If you're poaching for a crowd, poach eggs 30 minutes ahead and store in a bowl of cold water in the fridge. To serve, lower into simmering water for 30 seconds to reheat.
