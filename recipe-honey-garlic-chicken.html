Honey Garlic Butter Chicken Thighs

Pan-seared bone-in thighs with shatteringly crispy skin, finished in a glossy honey-garlic butter glaze. One skillet, 35 minutes, and it tastes like something from a proper restaurant.

About This Recipe

Chicken thighs are criminally underrated. They're cheaper than breasts, harder to overcook, and when you get that skin properly rendered and crispy, there's nothing quite like it. This recipe leans into everything that makes thighs great — a ripping-hot skillet to start, patience to let the fat render, and a glaze that clings to every inch of bronzed skin.

The honey-garlic butter sauce is built directly in the same pan after the chicken rests, using all those caramelized brown bits. A splash of soy sauce (use tamari to keep it gluten-free) adds depth and umami. The whole thing comes together in about five minutes while the chicken rests — timing is everything here.

Ingredients

  • 4 bone-in, skin-on chicken thighs (approx. 900g / 2 lbs)
  • 1 teaspoon kosher salt
  • ½ teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
  • ½ teaspoon smoked paprika
  • 1 tablespoon neutral oil (avocado or vegetable)
  • 4 tablespoons unsalted butter, divided
  • 6 garlic cloves, minced or pressed
  • 3 tablespoons honey
  • 2 tablespoons tamari or soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon apple cider vinegar
  • Fresh thyme sprigs and chopped parsley, to finish

Method

Dry and season the chicken
Pat the chicken thighs completely dry with paper towels — this is the single most important step for crispy skin. Season all over (including under the skin) with salt, pepper, and smoked paprika. Let them sit uncovered at room temperature for 15 minutes while you prepare everything else.
Sear skin-side down
Heat a heavy oven-safe skillet (cast iron works best) over medium-high heat until very hot. Add the oil and let it shimmer. Place the chicken thighs skin-side down — you'll hear a loud, satisfying sizzle. Do not move them for 12–14 minutes. Seriously. Resist the urge to check. You want the skin to render and turn deep golden brown before it releases.

Flip and finish in oven
Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C) while the chicken sears. Flip the thighs skin-side up. Add 2 tablespoons of butter and fresh thyme sprigs to the pan. Tilt the pan and baste the chicken with the melted butter. Transfer the skillet to the oven and roast for 10–12 minutes, until the internal temperature reaches 165°F (74°C).
Rest the chicken
Transfer the chicken to a plate or cutting board and tent loosely with foil. Rest for 5 minutes — don't skip this. The juices redistribute and the carry-over cooking finishes the job. Your kitchen will smell incredible at this point.
Build the honey garlic sauce
Return the skillet to medium heat. Add remaining 2 tablespoons butter and the minced garlic. Cook for 60–90 seconds, stirring constantly, until fragrant and just turning golden. Add honey, tamari, and apple cider vinegar. Stir and let it bubble for 2–3 minutes until slightly thickened and glossy. Taste and adjust — more honey for sweetness, more vinegar for tang.
Glaze and serve
Nestle the rested chicken back into the sauce and spoon the glaze generously over each thigh. Scatter with fresh parsley. Serve immediately with steamed rice, mashed potatoes, or crusty bread to soak up the extra sauce.

Chef's Tip: The key to non-soggy chicken skin is starting with a bone-dry surface and a screaming-hot pan. Any moisture on the skin will steam rather than sear. If you have time, season the chicken and refrigerate it uncovered overnight — this dry-brine technique results in the crispiest skin possible.
