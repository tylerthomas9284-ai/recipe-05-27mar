Roasted Mediterranean Vegetable Bowl

Charred zucchini, sweet peppers, and crispy chickpeas over herbed couscous, finished with a bright lemony tahini drizzle. The meal-prep bowl that actually tastes better the next day.

About This Recipe

This bowl started as our answer to the eternal "what do I meal prep on Sunday" question. Roasting transforms humble vegetables — even ones that have been sitting in the fridge a few days — into something deeply savory and caramelized. The high heat is key: 425°F, single layer, not crowded. Crowded vegetables steam; roasted vegetables char.

The tahini dressing is where the magic happens. Lemon juice causes tahini to seize up and go thick at first — keep whisking, add water gradually, and it suddenly transforms into a pourable, silky sauce. It keeps in the fridge for a week and goes with everything from roasted veg to grain salads to falafel.

Ingredients

  • 1 medium zucchini, cut into half-moons
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced into strips
  • 1 yellow bell pepper, sliced into strips
  • 1 red onion, cut into wedges
  • 1 can (400g) chickpeas, drained, rinsed, and dried
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil, divided
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
  • ½ teaspoon garlic powder
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 cup (175g) couscous
  • 1¼ cup boiling vegetable broth
  • ¼ cup fresh parsley or mint, roughly chopped

Method

Preheat and prep
Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C) with two oven racks positioned in the upper and lower thirds. Line two large baking sheets with parchment. Pat the drained chickpeas very dry with paper towels — the dryer they are, the crispier they'll get.
Season and roast
Spread the vegetables on one baking sheet and the chickpeas on the other. Drizzle each with olive oil, then season the vegetables with salt, pepper, and half the spice mix. Season the chickpeas with the remaining spice mix plus a generous pinch of salt. Toss everything separately to coat. Make sure nothing is overlapping — space is essential for char, not steam. Roast for 20–25 minutes, rotating pans halfway through.

Make the tahini dressing
While the vegetables roast, whisk together 3 tablespoons tahini, 2 tablespoons fresh lemon juice, 1 minced garlic clove, and a pinch of salt. The mixture will thicken dramatically — this is normal. Add cold water 1 tablespoon at a time, whisking continuously, until the dressing is creamy and pourable. It should coat a spoon but drip off easily. Adjust lemon and salt to taste.
Cook the couscous
Place couscous in a heatproof bowl. Pour the boiling vegetable broth over it, add ½ tablespoon olive oil and a pinch of salt. Stir, cover tightly with a plate or plastic wrap, and leave for exactly 5 minutes. Fluff with a fork, then fold in fresh parsley or mint and a squeeze of lemon juice.
Assemble and serve
Divide the herbed couscous between two wide bowls. Pile the roasted vegetables generously over the top. Add a handful of crispy chickpeas. Drizzle the tahini dressing liberally over everything. Finish with extra fresh herbs, a sprinkle of sumac if you have it, and a final drizzle of good olive oil.

Meal Prep: Store components separately in airtight containers for up to 4 days. Reheat vegetables and chickpeas in the oven at 375°F for 8 minutes to restore crispiness — don't microwave the chickpeas or they'll go soft. Make the dressing fresh or thin it with a splash of water before using from the fridge.
