Creamy Lemon Ricotta Pasta

Silky whole-milk ricotta, bright lemon zest, and a handful of fresh herbs transform five pantry ingredients into the ultimate weeknight comfort bowl. No heavy cream needed.

About This Recipe

This pasta came out of a Tuesday evening with an almost-empty fridge and a container of ricotta that needed using. What started as desperation cooking became one of the most-saved recipes on Hearth & Table. The secret is using the pasta cooking water — starchy, salty, and warm — to loosen the ricotta into a sauce that coats every strand without a drop of cream.

It's the kind of recipe that feels like it should be more complicated than it is. Five ingredients, one pot, twenty-five minutes. The lemon zest does all the heavy lifting in the flavor department — use a fresh lemon, not bottled juice, and don't skip zesting right before you eat.

Ingredients

  • 200g (7 oz) dried spaghetti or linguine
  • ¾ cup (180g) whole-milk ricotta, room temperature
  • Zest and juice of 1 large lemon
  • 2 tablespoons extra-virgin olive oil
  • ¼ cup freshly grated Parmesan, plus more to serve
  • 2 garlic cloves, finely grated
  • Handful of fresh basil or flat-leaf parsley
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper
  • Chili flakes, to taste (optional)

Method

Boil the pasta
Bring a large pot of well-salted water to a rolling boil. Add the pasta and cook according to package directions until al dente — usually 9–11 minutes for spaghetti. Before draining, scoop out 1 full cup of the starchy pasta water and set aside.
Build the ricotta sauce
While the pasta cooks, add ricotta, lemon zest, lemon juice, grated garlic, olive oil, and Parmesan to a large bowl. Season generously with salt and pepper. Stir until well combined — the mixture will look thick at this stage.

Combine pasta and sauce
Drain the pasta and immediately add it to the bowl with the ricotta mixture. Add ¼ cup of the reserved pasta water and toss vigorously. The heat from the pasta will warm the ricotta and the starchy water will transform it into a silky, glossy sauce. Add more pasta water, a splash at a time, until the sauce coats every strand beautifully.
Finish and plate
Taste and adjust seasoning — you may want more lemon juice for brightness or more salt to balance. Divide between two warmed bowls. Top with torn fresh basil or parsley, an extra grating of Parmesan, a drizzle of your best olive oil, and a pinch of chili flakes if using. Serve immediately.

Chef's Tip: The single most important thing in this recipe is reserving that pasta water before you drain. Don't skip it — it's the emulsifier that turns the thick ricotta mixture into a velvety sauce. If you forget and the sauce is too thick, a splash of warm water from the tap will work in a pinch, but the pasta water is superior.

Make it Heartier: Add a handful of baby spinach or arugula to the bowl before tossing — the heat from the pasta wilts it perfectly. Pan-fried shrimp or crispy pancetta are also excellent additions.
