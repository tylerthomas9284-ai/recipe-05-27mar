Cookie Policy

Last updated: March 23, 2026

This Cookie Policy explains how Hearth & Table ("we," "us," "our") uses cookies and similar tracking technologies on hearthandtable.site. By using our website, you consent to the use of cookies as described in this policy.

1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files stored on your device (computer, smartphone, or tablet) when you visit a website. They help websites remember your preferences, analyze traffic patterns, and deliver a more personalized experience. Cookies cannot execute code or transmit viruses.

2. Types of Cookies We Use

2.1 Strictly Necessary Cookies

These cookies are essential for the website to function correctly. They cannot be disabled. Examples include session cookies that maintain your login state and cookies that remember your cookie consent preference.

2.2 Performance & Analytics Cookies

These cookies collect anonymous information about how visitors use our site — which pages are visited most often, how long visitors stay, and where they come from. This data helps us improve the site experience. We use Google Analytics for this purpose.

2.3 Functional Cookies

Functional cookies allow the website to remember choices you make (such as your dietary preference filter or saved recipes) and provide enhanced, personalized features. These cookies may be set by us or by third-party providers whose services we use.

2.4 Targeting & Advertising Cookies

These cookies track your browsing habits across websites to build a profile of your interests and show you relevant advertising. We use these cookies on our free tier to deliver contextual recipe-related advertising. Premium subscribers can opt out of advertising cookies in their account settings.

4. Third-Party Cookies

Some cookies on our site are set by third-party services that appear on our pages. We do not control these cookies. Third parties include:

  • Google Analytics — website usage analytics
  • Google Fonts — font delivery (no personal data collected)
  • Unsplash — image delivery via CDN

5. Managing Cookies

You can control cookies in the following ways:

  • Browser Settings: Most browsers allow you to refuse cookies, delete existing cookies, or be notified when cookies are set. Consult your browser's help documentation for instructions.
  • Our Cookie Banner: Use the "Decline" button on our cookie consent banner to opt out of non-essential cookies.
  • Opt-Out Tools: You can opt out of Google Analytics tracking at tools.google.com/dlpage/gaoptout.

Please note that disabling certain cookies may affect the functionality of our website, including your ability to save recipes, maintain login sessions, or use dietary filters.

6. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Cookie Policy from time to time. Any changes will be posted on this page with an updated date. We encourage you to review this policy periodically.
